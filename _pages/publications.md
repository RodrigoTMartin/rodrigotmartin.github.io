## Work in Progress

**Import Restrictions and Labor Productivity: Evidence from Argentina** — *Submitted*
[[Paper]](https://repositorio.udesa.edu.ar/items/15774d1c-667a-4b1d-aaa0-a9ebbf851274)

<details>
<summary>Abstract</summary>
<p>This paper studies how Argentina's non-automatic import licenses (NAILs) affected manufacturing firms using ENDEI 2010–2012. A 10-percentage-point rise in sector–year exposure reduces importers' labor productivity by about 0.36% relative to non-importers in the same sector–year. The channel runs through sales: firms reliant on regional suppliers contract, whereas firms sourcing from distant markets show no detectable response. Employment is broadly flat, with modest within-firm reallocation patterns.</p>
</details>

## Working Papers

**Panorama de la Complejidad Económica del Municipio de Córdoba**
(with Paula Luvini and Constanza Cejas)
[[Paper]](https://fund.ar/publicacion/complejidad-economica-del-municipio-de-cordoba/)

<details>
<summary>Abstract</summary>
<p>This paper applies the economic complexity methodology to analyze the productive structure of the municipality of Córdoba, Argentina — the country's second largest city and a key technology and education hub. Using firm-level and international trade data, we characterize the city's export complexity, identify sectors with revealed comparative advantages, and detect opportunities for productive diversification based on proximity in the product space.</p>
</details>

**Elaboración de Modelos de Política Monetaria: Una Aproximación Empírica**
(with Cintia Martinez, Gonzalo Respighi and Silvia Vietri)
[[Paper]](https://ideas.repec.org/p/aep/anales/4576.html)

<details>
<summary>Abstract</summary>
<p>This paper studies monetary policy dynamics using VAR and SVAR models. Drawing on a multi-equation framework adapted from McCallum and Nelson (2010) for open economies, we examine the roles of the interest rate, money supply, exchange rate, and output growth in monetary policy design. We estimate the model using Chilean quarterly data (2011–2018) and identify the transmission mechanisms of monetary policy shocks through structural factorization techniques, cointegration analysis, and variance decomposition.</p>
</details>
